# Customer Drivers

## Business Drivers

- Improve the integrity of recruitment and payroll data across GPG.
- Prevent fraud in hiring, supplier engagement, and beneficiary administration.
- Support data-driven decision-making in service delivery programs.
- Reduce audit exposure and strengthen internal controls.
- Modernize government verification processes through biometric and demographic validation.

## Operational Pain Points

- Manual and fragmented verification processes
- Difficulty validating identities, addresses, banking details, and mortality status consistently
- Risk of civil servants engaging in business with the state without effective detection
- Poor data quality in payroll and beneficiary records
- Limited ability to provide timely, cross-department verification outcomes

## Governance and Control Expectations

- GPT wants central control of data access and system governance.
- Government must own the data, system artefacts, and source code.
- Hosting and storage must align to SITA and Department of e-Government governance requirements.
- Security controls must be visible, specific, and auditable.
- The customer expects the solution to fit public-sector operating controls, not impose a vendor-led operating model.

## Stakeholder Landscape

- Primary customer: Gauteng Provincial Treasury
- Government user groups: multiple provincial departments and municipalities
- Governance partners: Department of e-Government and SITA
- External information holders: DHA, credit bureaus, banking sector / provincial banker
- Internal support audience: GPT IT Services as first-line support

## Preferred Buying Posture Inferred from the RFP

- Compliance-first rather than innovation-first
- High emphasis on control, documentation, and operational accountability
- Preference for proven enterprise capability and demonstrable delivery maturity
- Strong concern about security, privacy, and audit outcomes
- Value-for-money orientation with strict limits on licensing and vendor lock-in

## What the Customer Will Likely Respond To

- Clear proof that the bidder understands public-sector governance realities
- A solution narrative that balances security, control, and operational usability
- Explicit handling of DHA, credit bureau, banking, and SITA dependencies
- Confidence that the bidder can deliver the prototype and the 12-month implementation window
- Language that respects GPT ownership, accountability, and decision rights
