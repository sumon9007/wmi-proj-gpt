# Project Charter

## Project Title

Development of Biometric Authentication System Including Support and Maintenance for a Period of 3 Years

## Customer and Sponsor Context

- Customer: Gauteng Provincial Treasury (GPT), on behalf of Gauteng Provincial Government (GPG)
- Delivery context: Public-sector implementation with centralized governance, regulated data handling, and multi-department usage
- Operating environment: SITA-managed Windows hosting environment with Department of e-Government support

## Business Objective

Design, build, deploy, and support a biometric-driven authentication platform that improves recruitment screening, payroll cleansing, fraud prevention, supplier due diligence, and beneficiary verification through real-time identity and demographic validation.

## Scope Summary

### In Scope

- Web-based biometric authentication platform
- Desktop and mobile-capable user experience
- DHA integration for identity verification
- Credit bureau integration for demographic, employment, address, and business-interest verification
- Banking validation support
- Persal-related payroll integration support
- Reporting, dashboards, audit trails, and exception reporting
- ESB / integration layer
- Training, documentation, first-line support enablement, and operational handover
- Support and maintenance for the remaining term after implementation

### Out of Scope or Not Yet Defined

- Final pricing and commercial approval artifacts
- Named customer-side resource assignments
- Detailed banking and payroll interface specifications
- Final environment configuration details from SITA / GPT

## Success Criteria

- Solution designed, built, tested, deployed, and accepted within the first 12 months
- Support and maintenance operating model established for months 13-36
- Core verification workflows operating across agreed interfaces
- Security, privacy, audit, and resilience controls aligned to tender requirements
- GPT IT Services trained and enabled for first-line support
- Documentation, source code, and related artefacts ready for GPT-controlled handover

## Governance Overview

- GPT acts as customer, data owner, and access-control authority
- Department of e-Government supports governance, security, and hosting alignment
- SITA provides or governs the target hosting environment
- Delivery governance should include:
  - project working group
  - architecture / security review cadence
  - dependency and risk review
  - milestone acceptance reviews

## Key Assumptions

- GPT will provide approved IP and access arrangements required for DHA connectivity
- SITA-managed target hosting environment will be made available in line with the plan
- Third-party integration arrangements can be enabled within delivery timelines
- Delivery may be performed through a hybrid model of on-site and VPN-enabled remote work

## Key Constraints

- Implementation, deployment, and training must complete within 12 months of project start
- Support and maintenance continue through month 36
- No third-party license cost may be charged for use of the delivered platform
- All data and project-generated IP vest exclusively in GPT
- Hosting must remain within a SITA-managed Windows environment
- DR, uptime, and security controls must meet explicit tender thresholds
