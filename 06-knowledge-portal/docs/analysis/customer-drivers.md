# Customer Drivers

## Business Drivers

- improve the integrity of recruitment and payroll data
- prevent fraud in hiring, supplier engagement, and beneficiary administration
- support data-driven decision-making in public service delivery
- reduce audit exposure and strengthen internal controls
- modernize government verification processes through biometric and demographic validation

## Operational Pain Points

- manual and fragmented verification processes
- difficulty validating identities, addresses, banking details, and mortality status consistently
- weak visibility into business-interest conflicts involving public servants
- poor data quality in payroll and beneficiary records
- limited cross-department consistency and reporting

## Governance Expectations

- GPT wants central control of data access and system governance
- government ownership of data and artefacts is mandatory
- hosting and storage must fit SITA and Department of e-Government expectations
- security controls must be specific, visible, and auditable

## Buying Posture

The customer is likely to respond best to a compliance-first, control-oriented proposal that shows proven delivery maturity, not just innovation language. Clarity, governance fit, and operational realism matter.
