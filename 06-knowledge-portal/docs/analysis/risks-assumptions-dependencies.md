# Risks, Assumptions, and Dependencies

## Key Risks

- DHA connectivity through GPT-approved IP and access arrangements may be delayed
- SITA-managed hosting readiness may affect the implementation critical path
- banking and payroll-related interface definitions may be incomplete at project start
- security and resilience controls may require more effort than a baseline build plan assumes
- cross-department stakeholder decisions may slow design validation and sign-off

## Core Assumptions

- GPT will provide approved network, environment, and access arrangements
- DHA and other external integrations can be established under customer-controlled agreements
- customer governance stakeholders will be available for decisions and sign-off
- delivery can operate through a hybrid on-site and remote working model

## Critical Dependencies

- DHA network path and access approvals
- SITA environment provisioning and readiness
- credit bureau setup and onboarding
- banking validation interface clarification
- payroll or Persal-related integration clarification
- GPT IT participation in training and operational handover

## Why This Matters

These items sit on the real delivery path. They should be managed from mobilization onward and reflected in both implementation planning and governance reporting.
