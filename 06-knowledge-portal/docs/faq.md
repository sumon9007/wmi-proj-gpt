# FAQ

## What is the customer actually buying?

GPT is procuring a biometric authentication platform that supports recruitment, payroll cleansing, supplier screening, beneficiary verification, and fraud prevention across Gauteng government structures.

## Is this just a biometric front end?

No. The opportunity is broader than device capture. It includes integration with DHA, credit bureau services, banking validation, payroll-related workflows, reporting, audit trails, governance controls, and long-term support.

## Where must the solution be hosted?

The solution must align to a SITA-managed Windows-based environment. The design should not assume unrestricted vendor-managed hosting.

## Who owns the data and source code?

GPT is expected to own the operational data, source code, configuration, and project-generated IP.

## Is licensing a major commercial constraint?

Yes. The tender does not allow third-party license costs for use of the delivered system. That constraint affects platform choices and commercial structure.

## Why is DHA such a big dependency?

DHA is the most critical external verification source. Prototype readiness and production readiness both depend on approved connectivity, network controls, and access arrangements under GPT governance.

## What external integrations matter most?

The most important are DHA, credit bureau services, banking validation, and payroll or Persal-related interfaces.

## What is Waymark Infotech's role?

Waymark Infotech is the bidder and proposed implementation partner. The company is positioning itself as the systems integrator responsible for solution delivery, integration, prototype readiness, handover, and support over the contract term.

## Why does the proposal emphasize governance so strongly?

Because this is a public-sector control platform. Security, auditability, data ownership, operational accountability, and policy alignment are part of the value proposition, not just compliance overhead.

## What should a new team member understand first?

Three things:

- the project is governance-heavy, not just feature-heavy
- customer-controlled dependencies can affect the critical path
- the 12-month implementation window means environment, integration, and prototype risks must be surfaced early
