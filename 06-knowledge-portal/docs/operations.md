# Operations

## Operating Model

The solution is intended to run as a government-controlled service rather than a vendor-controlled platform. GPT retains ownership of data, source code, configuration, and project-generated IP, while the operational environment remains aligned to SITA and Department of e-Government governance.

## Hosting Responsibilities

- SITA-managed environment provides the target hosting model
- GPT governs access, data ownership, and operating policy
- Department of e-Government supports governance and security alignment
- Waymark Infotech provides implementation, support, and transition support during the contract term

## Support And Maintenance Model

The operational plan assumes:

- first 12 months focused on delivery and stabilization
- next 24 months focused on support, maintenance, updates, and operational continuity
- GPT IT Services prepared for first-line support
- escalation paths defined for application, integration, environment, and security issues

## Security And Monitoring Expectations

Operational controls expected by the tender include:

- RBAC and MFA enforcement
- encryption in transit and at rest
- audit logging for user and admin activity
- monitoring and alerting for availability and security events
- incident response and escalation procedures
- periodic review of access, logs, and service health

## SLA And Resilience Expectations

The service model must support:

- 99.5% uptime excluding scheduled maintenance
- disaster recovery aligned to RTO of 4 hours
- disaster recovery aligned to RPO of 15 minutes
- backup and restore procedures
- semi-annual DR testing

## Handover And Transition

Operational handover is a formal workstream, not an informal closeout activity. GPT IT should receive:

- system administration guidance
- first-line support procedures
- escalation paths
- operational documentation
- reporting and monitoring guidance
- access to required deployment and configuration artefacts
