# Project Context

## Customer
Gauteng Provincial Treasury (GPT), on behalf of Gauteng Provincial Government (GPG)

## Project Name
Development of Biometric Authentication System Including Support and Maintenance for a Period of 3 Years

## Engagement Type
Implementation with support and maintenance

## Project Status
Tender / pre-award opportunity

## Business Objective
Design, build, deploy, and support a biometric-driven authentication platform that improves recruitment screening, payroll cleansing, fraud prevention, supplier due diligence, and beneficiary verification across Gauteng government departments through real-time identity and demographic validation.

## Technical Scope
Develop a web-based and mobile-capable biometric authentication system integrated with Department of Home Affairs services, credit bureau services, banking verification, and Persal-related workflows. The solution must support fingerprint and facial verification, real-time and bulk queries, role-based access, reporting, audit trails, ESB-based integration, secure hosting in a SITA-managed Windows environment, and compliance with GPT security and data-governance standards.

## Key Deliverables
- Web-based biometric authentication system with desktop and mobile capability
- Integration layer / ESB for DHA, credit bureau, banking-sector, and internal government systems
- Identity, banking, address, and mortality verification workflows
- Reporting, dashboards, exception reports, and audit trail capability
- Product requirements, system description, software architecture, source code, and user documentation
- Training, first-line support enablement, and 3-year maintenance / support plan

## Compliance Requirements
- POPIA
- PFMA and Treasury regulations
- SITA Act requirements
- ISO/IEC 27001-aligned security controls
- GPT and Department of e-Government security and data governance directives

## Key Stakeholders
- Gauteng Provincial Treasury (customer, data owner, access-control authority)
- Gauteng Provincial Government departments and municipalities (business users)
- Gauteng Department of e-Government (security, hosting, governance support)
- SITA (managed hosting environment)
- Department of Home Affairs (identity verification source)
- Credit bureau providers (demographic, employment, address, and business-interest verification)
- Banking sector / provincial banker (banking validation)

## Current Challenges
- Manual and fragmented verification processes across recruitment, payroll, supply chain, and beneficiary screening
- Fraud risk and weak controls around identity, business-interest, and beneficiary validation
- Need for accurate demographic, employment, banking, and mortality data in real time
- Cross-department integration complexity across government and private-sector information holders
- High expectations for data protection, operational resilience, and auditability in a public-sector environment

## Known Assumptions
- GPT will provide approved IP addresses, data storage, and policy-aligned access into the target environment
- DHA, credit bureau, banking, and related external integrations can be established under GPT/GPG agreements
- System development can occur on GPT premises or remotely via VPN access
- GPT intends to centrally control roles, access policies, and data ownership across participating departments

## Known Constraints
- Development and deployment, including user training, must be completed within the first 12 months of the 3-year contract
- Remaining 2 years are reserved for support, maintenance, and updates
- No third-party license costs may be charged for system use
- All IP, source code, configuration, and project artefacts must vest exclusively in GPT
- Hosting must be within a SITA-managed cloud / Windows-based environment
- Security controls must include encryption at rest and in transit, RBAC, MFA, audit logging, backup, DR, and incident response
- DR targets are explicit: RTO of 4 hours and RPO of 15 minutes
- Service availability target is 99.5% uptime excluding scheduled maintenance

## Timeline Expectations
- start date: after tender award / contract signature
- target completion: implementation and go-live within 12 months of contract start; support and maintenance continue through month 36
